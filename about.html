About This Project

Our Mission

In an increasingly globalized world, understanding different communication styles is essential for effective cross-cultural interaction. This assessment tool was created to help students and professionals recognize their own communication preferences and learn how to adapt when interacting with people from different cultural backgrounds.

The Science Behind It

Our assessment is based on established research in intercultural communication, particularly the work of anthropologist Edward T. Hall on high-context and low-context cultures. The questionnaire measures preferences across key dimensions:

  • Directness: How explicitly you communicate your messages
  • Context Sensitivity: Your awareness of non-verbal cues and implicit meaning
  • Relationship vs. Task Orientation: Whether you prioritize relationships or goals
  • Conflict Resolution Style: How you approach disagreements
  • Time Perception: Your attitude toward punctuality and deadlines

How to Use Your Results

Your assessment results provide insights into your natural communication tendencies. Use them to:

  • Understand your strengths and potential blind spots
  • Recognize when you might need to adapt your style
  • Develop greater cultural awareness and flexibility
  • Improve communication in diverse teams and settings
